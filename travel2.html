PIONEERS BEYOND EARTH

ASTRONAUTS

Rakesh Sharma (India)

First Indian in space (1984) aboard Soviet Soyuz T-11. Famous for saying "Saare Jahan Se Achha" from space.

Kalpana Chawla (India)

First Indian-born woman in space. Flew on Columbia shuttle; tragically died in 2003 during re-entry.

Sunita Williams (India/USA)

American astronaut of Indian origin. Spent 321 days in space. Known for spacewalks and ISS missions.

Raja Chari (India/USA)

NASA astronaut of Indian origin. Pilot and commander in NASA’s Artemis and SpaceX Crew missions.

Gaganyaan Astronauts (India)

Four IAF pilots selected for ISRO’s upcoming Gaganyaan mission — India’s first human spaceflight project.

Yuri Gagarin (Russia)

First human in space, 1961. Orbited Earth on Vostok 1, marking the beginning of human space exploration.

Neil Armstrong (USA)

First person to walk on the Moon (1969) during Apollo 11 mission. Famous for "one small step" quote.

Buzz Aldrin (USA)

Second person to walk on the Moon. Lunar Module pilot for Apollo 11, known for his contributions to lunar science.

Valentina Tereshkova (Russia)

First woman in space (1963). Flew solo in Vostok 6. Remains the only woman to fly solo in space.

Chris Hadfield (Canada)

Famous Canadian astronaut. Commander of ISS, known for his space guitar performance and outreach.

Peggy Whitson (USA)

Holds record for most time spent in space by an American astronaut (665 days). ISS commander multiple times.

Mae Jemison (USA)

First African-American woman in space (1992). Mission specialist on STS-47 Endeavour.

John Glenn (USA)

First American to orbit Earth (1962). Returned to space at age 77 on Shuttle Discovery in 1998.

Sally Ride (USA)

First American woman in space (1983). A physicist and mission specialist aboard Challenger STS-7.

Alan Shepard (USA)

First American in space (1961) and fifth person to walk on the Moon during Apollo 14 mission.
