Space Phenomena

Eclipses

Solar eclipses occur when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, casting a shadow on Earth. Lunar eclipses happen when the Earth is between the Sun and the Moon, causing the Moon to appear red.

Fun Fact: A total solar eclipse can last up to 7.5 minutes!

Phases of the Moon

The Moon has eight phases: New Moon, Waxing Crescent, First Quarter, Waxing Gibbous, Full Moon, Waning Gibbous, Last Quarter, and Waning Crescent.

Fun Fact: It takes the Moon about 29.5 days to complete its cycle of phases.

Supermoon

A supermoon occurs when the Moon is at its closest point to Earth in its orbit, making it appear larger and brighter than usual.

Fun Fact: A supermoon can appear up to 14% larger and 30% brighter than a regular full moon.

Auroras

Auroras, or Northern and Southern Lights, are natural light displays caused by charged particles from the Sun interacting with Earth’s atmosphere.

Fun Fact: The colors of auroras depend on the type of gas in the atmosphere—oxygen produces green and red, while nitrogen creates blue and purple hues.
